Roomr Corporate Room Reservation App

Challenge

The current conference room reservation system is outdated and only available on desktop. The system does not give feedback on available rooms, nor does it follow the naming conventions for each office building. Employees are frustrated by the system and often skip reserving the room.

Our project was a culmination of a three week long intern project for AT& T in summer 2014.

Project Description

Our goal was to make an android app for AT& T employees to reserve conference rooms at any building in the nation. Additional features we included are the ability to sync the app to Outlook & invite other users.

Initial Sketching

Due to the limited time frame of our project, preliminary research was restricted to a review of the current scheduling site and talking to employees in the Bothell office about their experiences with it. We then conducted a fast paced 'group think' sketching session before I dove into prototyping.

Initial Sketching

Our sketching session was fast paced and used more as inspiration for the prototyping phase.

Prototyping

Our prototype was made in Axure using style guidelines from the Ratchet development library.

Development

Screens from the prototype were printed out and turned into a web of interactions. This board became our location for daily scrum meetings and a type of library for interns doing the development, where they could 'check out' screens or add physical comments for design considerations. We chose a physical board instead of an online collaboration space due to company-wide software restrictions.
